# Waterflow Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 29 April 2026_

Waterflow is an iOS app for viewing public water station data from the U.S. Geological Survey (USGS). This policy explains what information the app does and does not handle.

## Data the app does not collect

Waterflow does not collect, store, or transmit personal information. Specifically, the app does not:

- Require an account or sign-in
- Use analytics or tracking SDKs
- Share or sell any data to third parties
- Display advertising

## Location

If you grant Waterflow access to your location, it is used **only on your device** to identify nearby water stations and to center the map. Your location is never transmitted to me or to any third party. You can revoke location access at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services → Waterflow.

## Public data sources

Waterflow fetches publicly available data from:

- The [USGS Water Services API](https://waterservices.usgs.gov/) for station readings.
- The [Iowa Environmental Mesonet (IEM) NEXRAD service](https://mesonet.agron.iastate.edu/) for radar imagery.

These requests contain only the information needed to fetch the data (e.g. station IDs, map tile coordinates) and your device's IP address, which those services may log under their own policies.

## Data stored on your device

Your favorited stations and app preferences are stored locally on your device. If you have iCloud enabled, this information may sync across your Apple devices through Apple's iCloud service, governed by [Apple's privacy policy](https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/).

## Children

Waterflow is suitable for general audiences and does not knowingly collect information from children.
